The MK5 GTI

  • 2.0T
  • 6 speed manual or dual clutch
  • 3025lbs.
  • 200 horsepower
  • 200lbft torque

The MK5 GTI was released in 2006 in North America and was the successor to the MK4. This GTI continues the smooth styling of its last 3 predecessors, and now features a different and unique front end to differentiate it from the rest of the Golf lineup of that generation, with a GTI badge on the front similar to the MK1 and MK2. This GTI did away with the VR6 and opted for a 2.0L turbocharged inline 4, similar to the 1.8T. This was known as the 2.0T. The engine is just 1 of the important new features of this car. The MK5 not only came with a 6 speed manual gearbox, but also a dual-clutch transmission which allowed for fast and smooth shifts and feels similar to an automatic transmission.
