What is a GTI?

The Volkswagen Golf GTI is a compact hatchback produced by Volkswagen from 1976 to today. GTI stands for Grand Tourer Injection, highlighting its use of direct fuel injection, which back in the 70s was a big deal. Not so much anymore. It is famous for being one of the first affordable cars with a higher performance and a lower price tag. It also began a new type of car, the ‘hot hatch,’ which is a hatchback car with a focus on performance. The GTI’s lightweight body and powerful engine and low cost made it a game changer in the automotive industry, and would pave the way for cars just like it. Today, the GTI is still a great choice for a hatchback with power, even with other auto companies releasing cars similar to it.
